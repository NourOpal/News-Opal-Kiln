D / Resources Where to dig deeper

Eyecandy — places to source the look.

Curated archives for footage, performance, and visual systems. Skip the AI/stock-heavy stuff. These are where the actual cinematic and the actual weird live.

Artgrid — my collectionartgrid.io

Sister to Artlist but actually cinematic. Even though it's still stock, the curation is so much better — real DPs, real frames. The collection link above shows the exact Artgrid pulls that match the world half. Use as a visual reference even if we don't license from there.
Internet Archivearchive.org

Public domain home movies, 8mm/Super 8 transfers, vintage film, ephemeral footage. Specific collections worth digging: Prelinger Home Movies, A/V Geeks, Center for Home Movies, Texas Archive of the Moving Image. Free, downloadable, no licensing.
UbuWeb — film + videoubu.com/film

Performance art archive. Avant-garde film, sound poetry, conceptual works, art documentation from the 1900s onward. The "Film, performance art, people winning awards for making art" beat lives here.
My codeart tool — WIPnourbenameur-codeart.vercel.app

A generative-art / object-detection tool I'm building. Loads any photo or video and overlays it with COCO-SSD bounding boxes + AI-generated italic poetry ("Every pixel holds a ghost" / "The algorithm remembers you" / "Glitched out like we never happened"). Comes with multiple visual modes — Dragon Curve, Clifford Attractor, Harmonograph, Lissajous Curves, Moiré Interference, ASCII Art, Blob Track, Pixel Mosaic, Text Silhouette, Body Particles. Especially good for the algo half of the film — literally rendering "the algorithm watching." Still WIP; happy to add modes the team needs. Drop in a still or clip and play with the parameters (Class Filter, Light Filter, Threshold, Max Blobs, Box Scale, Lines, Text Size, Color, Seed). Controls: pan, double-click resets, space plays, R randomises, S symmetry, D scroll mode.
